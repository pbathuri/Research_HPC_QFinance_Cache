# Proposal to Paper Bridge

## Original proposal intent

- Finance-centered workloads
- cache/reuse-oriented systems questions
- eventual HPC/QHPC relevance

## Why evolution occurred

- The project moved to staged empirical buildout before architecture claims.
- This reduced over-claim risk and improved methodological defensibility.

## What stayed constant

- Finance workload grounding (event/panel/risk/pricing)
- cache/reuse as the core systems theme
- HPC/QHPC relevance as a later-stage objective

## What changed

- Added unified observability and claim-typed hypothesis layers.
- Framed guided-cache architecture as hypothesis, not deployed controller.

## What is deferred

- PMU-backed microarchitectural validation
- BigRed200-scale behavior confirmation
- deeper quantum/HPC coupling experiments

## Why the current paper remains faithful

- It answers core proposal questions with stronger evidence discipline.
- It documents what is known now and what must be validated later.
